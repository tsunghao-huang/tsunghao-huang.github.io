Here's a time line of RATM

  • 1991 - Rage Against the Machine is born. They gave the first public performance at the Quad of California State University, Northridge
  • 1992 - The band released the eponymous debut studio album, Rage Against the Machine, which peaked at number 1 on the US Billboard Heatseekers chart.
  • 1996 - Second album, Evil Empire, entered Billboard's Top 200 chart at number one in 1996, and subsequently rose to triple platinum status.
  • 1997 - The band opened for U2 on their PopMart Tour, for which all of Rage's profits went to support social organizations
  • 1999 - The Battle of Los Angeles debuted at number one in 1999, selling 450,000 copies in the first week and then going double-platinum. That same year the song "Wake Up" was featured on the soundtrack of the film The Matrix.
  • 2000 - On January 26, 2000, during filming of the music video for "Sleep Now in the Fire", directed by Michael Moore, the band caused the doors of the New York Stock Exchange to be closed. In the same year on October 18, the band broke up after vocalist Zack de la Rocha released a statement announcing his departure from the band.
  • 2003 - The Battle of Los Angeles was ranked number 426 on Rolling Stone's list of the 500 greatest albums of all time.
  • 2007 - The band reunited and perform at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival. The members continued to tour in the United States, New Zealand, Australia, and Japan
  • 2008 - Rage performed at the Target Center in Minneapolis during the Republican National Convention.
  • 2009 - "Killing in the Name" became the number-one single in the UK for Christmas 2009.
  • 2010 - Rage headlined European festivals in June 2010 including the Download Festival at Donington Park, England, Rock am Ring and Rock im Park in Germany and Rock in Rio Madrid in Spain.
  • 2019 - It was reported that RATM were reuniting for their first shows in nine years in the spring of 2020.
  • 2020 - Rage Against the Machine announced more worldwide dates for the 2020 reunion tour, now named the Public Service Announcement Tour. On May 1, 2020, the band announced that they had rescheduled the remaining dates of their reunion tour to 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
For more information, please refer to their Wikipedia page.
